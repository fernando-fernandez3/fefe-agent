# Knowledge: ds_spanish_fluency — Spanish Fluency by End of Year

> Research-backed decomposition for building Spanish fluency with explicit separation between SLA evidence and practical operating choices.
> Last updated: 2026-04-22

---

## Goal Definition

Spanish fluency here means meaningful conversational, listening, reading, and writing ability that is visible in real tasks, not just app streaks.

## What the sources directly support

### 1. Comprehensible input matters
Krashen makes the case for large amounts of understandable input.

**Implication:** Listening and reading volume have to be core, not optional extras.

### 2. Output matters too
Swain's work is the corrective. Learners need to produce language and notice gaps.

**Implication:** Speaking and writing practice must be part of the system, not something postponed until “later.”

### 3. Vocabulary growth benefits from deliberate review
Nation and spacing research support explicit lexical work and spaced retrieval.

**Implication:** A fluency plan should include high-frequency vocabulary plus spaced review.

### 4. Balanced programs outperform one-strand obsession
Nation's Four Strands says a healthy language program balances meaning-focused input, meaning-focused output, language-focused learning, and fluency development.

**Implication:** Do not over-index on only conversation, only flashcards, or only passive media.

### 5. Fluency needs externalized standards
ACTFL, CEFR, and DELE all exist to prevent vague self-delusion.

**Implication:** Progress should be checked against functional descriptors, not just “I feel better at Spanish.”

## Operational subdomains

### 1. Comprehensible input volume
What to track:
- weekly listening hours
- weekly reading volume
- whether materials are mostly understandable

### 2. Output practice
What to track:
- speaking sessions
- writing sessions
- correction or feedback loop

### 3. Vocabulary and retrieval
What to track:
- high-frequency vocabulary growth
- spaced-review consistency
- words that have moved from recognition to use

### 4. Externalized assessment
What to track:
- ACTFL or CEFR-aligned monthly check-ins
- can-do statements achieved
- DELE-style task readiness if useful

### 5. Balanced program design
What to track:
- whether all four strands are represented
- total weekly hours
- whether progress has stalled from over-reliance on one method

---

## Inference layer, explicit

These are practical choices, not direct mandates from one source:
- using B2-ish functional ability as the target shape
- monthly rather than quarterly proficiency check-ins
- setting specific weekly hour targets
- preferring family / work / daily-life topics as the first serious output domains

## Suggested thresholds, provisional

| Signal | Green | Yellow | Red |
|---|---|---|---|
| Weekly input | >= 3 hours | 1 to 3 hours | < 1 hour |
| Weekly output | >= 2 sessions | 1 session | 0 |
| SRS / vocab review | consistent | mixed | mostly absent |
| Monthly can-do check | completed | partial | skipped |
| Program balance | all four strands present | uneven | one-strand dominant |

## How Hermes should use this file

When evaluating Spanish-fluency opportunities:
1. Is this improving input, output, vocabulary, assessment, or balance?
2. Is it likely to create real language use, or just the feeling of studying?
3. What specific can-do statement should improve if this works?
4. Is the current bottleneck lack of volume, lack of output, lack of retrieval, or lack of honest assessment?

If a proposal does not improve one of those, it is probably fluff.
