## What the sources directly support

### 1. Friendship quality matters
Research on active-constructive responding and shared novelty shows that how partners respond to good moments and how they create shared positive experience both matter.

**Implication:** Being a great husband is not just about preventing conflict. It is also about actively deepening friendship and enjoyment.

### 2. Conflict competence matters
Gottman/Levenson and demand-withdraw work both show that harsh escalation and dysfunctional conflict loops predict worse outcomes.

**Implication:** Gentle starts, repair attempts, and avoiding pursue-withdraw spirals are core skills.

### 3. Responsiveness is a real construct, not a vibe
Intimacy science treats responsiveness as feeling understood, validated, and cared for.

**Implication:** Partnership quality includes whether Karina actually experiences Fernando as responsive, not just whether he believes he was trying.

### 4. Trust is built through dependability and predictability
Trust literature emphasizes consistency and reliability.

**Implication:** Follow-through and emotional steadiness count as marriage maintenance, not just logistics competence.

### 5. Maintenance behaviors and rituals matter
Relationship-maintenance and ritual research both support recurring small practices.

**Implication:** Weekly check-ins, date rituals, and appreciation habits are justified. They are not fluff.

## Operational subdomains

### 1. Friendship and fondness
What to track:
- appreciation
- active response to good news
- shared enjoyment
- sense of being known

### 2. Conflict and repair
What to track:
- gentle starts
- speed of repair after tension
- reduction in harsh escalation
- avoidance of demand-withdraw cycles

### 3. Responsiveness and trust
What to track:
- bids noticed and answered
- emotional validation
- follow-through on commitments
- reliability under stress

### 4. Rituals and maintenance
What to track:
- weekly check-ins
- at-home or out-of-home date rituals
- small thoughtful gestures
- recurring connection practices

### 5. Measurement and reflection
What to track:
- satisfaction trend
- whether connection rituals are actually happening
- whether Karina's lived experience matches Fernando's intention

## Inference layer, explicit

These are local operating choices:
- using at-home date nights as a major threshold because of childcare reality
- using a weekly check-in format with appreciation / needs / look-ahead
- treating thoughtful gestures as a formal maintenance category
- translating broad relationship science into a small household rhythm

All good, but still local.

This domain is observation and suggestion only.

Ask:
1. Is the current gap friendship, repair, responsiveness, trust, or ritual maintenance?
2. What small repeated action would improve the relationship this week?
3. Is the recommendation realistic inside their current family constraints?
